  • -4 Jul

    The Panamanian-flagged MV Wakashio departs from Lianyungang, China, bound for Tubarão, Brazil, with 20 crew onboard and no cargo, but having 3,894 tonnes of low-sulphur fuel oil, 207 tonnes of diesel and 90 tonnes of lube oil onboard.

  • -25 Jul

    The ship runs aground on a coral reef off the nation island of Mauritius in the Indian Ocean. There are no injuries and the crew remains onboard.

  • -6 Aug

    Oil begins to leak from the vessel, while Mauritius authorities are trying to control the spill.

  • -7 Aug

    The island of Mauritius declares a “state of environmental emergency”, fearing of huge damage to the country’s coastal waters and marine wildlife.

  • –10 Aug

    About 1,000 metric tons of fuel have already spilled, with estimates of the remaining oil onboard ranging from 2,500 to 3,000 metric tons. Meanwhile, inclement weather with high waves triggers worries of a potential vessel crack

  • -12 Aug

    In a race against time, an estimated 3,000 tonnes of fuel are successfully removed from the ship. The fuel is transferred to shore by helicopter and to another ship also owned by Nagashiki Shipping.

  • -15 Aug

    The ship breaks in two with 166 tons of fuel onboard. Waves 4.5 metres high hinder cleanup.

  • -22 Aug

    Operations to sink the forward part of the ill-fated vessel are completed, despite environmental concerns.

  • -26-28 Aug

    At least 40 dead dolphins gradually wash up on Mauritius’s shore, spurring suspicions that the oil spill is to blame, with officials characterizing the incident a “sad coincidence.”

  • -29 Aug

    Thousands of people demonstrate in Mauritius over the government’s handling of the incident.
